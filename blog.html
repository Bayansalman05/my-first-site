How my time at Salford Business School has prepared me for a career in business development

Bayan Salman · Business Development · April 2026

Business Development is not a role you can learn entirely from a textbook. It requires commercial instinct, the ability to read a room, and the confidence to present to people who have built entire careers doing what you are just beginning. The British University of Bahrain gave me something that no amount of theory alone could a structured opportunity to apply everything I was learning in real, high-stakes environments.

What Salford taught me

Salford did not just teach me applicable theory it built the practical toolkit I use every single day. I studied project management, business intelligence and dashboards, marketing campaigns and live simulations, strategic management, and innovation and entrepreneurship. Each module built a layer of practical competency that translated directly into my real-world roles.

The Bahrain Economic Development Board

In the summer before my final year, I interned at the Bahrain Economic Development Board as a Research Intern. I built Power BI dashboards to track FDI performance data, benchmarked Bahrain across global competitiveness indices, and presented findings directly to senior leadership. My business intelligence training at Salford meant I already understood how to structure a dashboard.

The BENEFIT Company: turning an idea into a boardroom proposal

At The BENEFIT Company, I was personally selected by the CEO to support a strategic innovation project. I proposed a new app feature, conducted full market research, and presented my recommendations to executive management. The innovation and entrepreneurship work I did at Salford taught me that a good idea without a rigorous business case is just a suggestion.

INJAZ Al-Arab Company of the Year

As CEO of Temprah, I led our brilliant team to win the INJAZ Al-Arab Company of the Year. Every module I studied at Salford showed up in that experience — from project management keeping operations on track, to strategic management shaping our competitive positioning.

Three digital marketing trends shaping my BD career

First, AI-powered personalisation is reshaping how BD professionals target and engage prospects. Second, LinkedIn has become the dominant engine for B2B networking across the GCC profiles with consistent content receive 40× more opportunities (LinkedIn, 2024). Third, data-driven decision making is now a baseline expectation for BD professionals, not a specialist skill.

What comes next

I am actively seeking Business Development roles in Bahrain the GCC and Europe with particular interest in FinTech, government-aligned organisations, and innovation-led sectors where Bahrain's Vision 2030 agenda is creating genuine growth opportunities.
